Film Terms and Concepts


Blocking, Staging, Framing

Blocking: The choreography of actors (where they stand, how they move, and when they cross)
Blocking focuses on the kinetic relationship between performers and their environment.  

Staging is a broader term that encompasses blocking plus the placement of 
set pieces, props, and camera angles. 

Framing refers specifically to how the camera captures that staged scene. 


Field Sizes

ECU - Extreme Close-Up: Focuses on a tiny detail (eye, mouth) for intensity.
CU  - Close-Up: Frames face from shoulders up for emotion.
MCU - Medium Close-Up: Frames chest/shoulders up; balances detail and context.
CS  - Close Shot: Tighter than MS, usually chest up.
MS  - Medium Shot: Frames subject from waist up; shows body language.
FS  - Full Shot: Displays entire body from head to toe.
LS  - Long Shot: Same as FS; emphasizes subject vs. environment.
ELS - Extreme Long Shot: Subject is small in a vast landscape; establishes scale.   

VLS - Very Long Shot: Shows a vast area where the subject is very small; emphasizes geography.
WS  - Wide Shot: Similar to LS; captures the subject and significant surroundings.
MWS - Medium Wide Shot: Frames subject from knees up; balances body language and setting.
BCU - Big Close-Up: Tighter than a standard CU; frames forehead to chin for intense emotion.
XLS - Extreme Long Shot: Alternative abbreviation for ELS; subject is tiny in a huge landscape.
CS  - Cowboy Shot (American Shot): Frames subject from mid-thighs up to include holsters/hands.
CH  - Choker Shot: Very tight close-up cutting off at the neck or above the eyebrows.
2S  - Two Shot: Frames two subjects in the same shot, usually from the waist up.
GV  - General View: Wide shots used to illustrate a location or subject contextually.
OTS - Over The Shoulder: Shot taken from behind one subject to look at another.
POV - Point Of View: Shot showing what a character is seeing.
